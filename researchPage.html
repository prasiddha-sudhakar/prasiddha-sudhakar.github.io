Behavioral Economics and Food Wastage

Background

"Don't waste food!" my parents would yell at my six year old self, as I tried to avoid eating the spinach (still do that now, sometimes)

But when someone asks me today, "What is the biggest problem in our world today?" my universal reply will be "Food Waste"

At Rutgers, I was able to take part in the Enactus Case: Social Entrepreneurship Competition where I led a team of students in coming up with a business idea that would solve food insecurity amongst the New Brunswick and Rutgers community.

Our slides from the competition can be viewed here

What I do

Through this competition, I was able to find INFEWS - a research group led by Dr Shwom and Dr Cuite.

I'm currently a research assistant at the Department of Human Ecology. The aim of our research group is to analyze the behaviors that have the greatest impacts on food, water and energy consumption. We will be analyzing how our feedback to households regarding their consumption patterns contribute to their change in behaviors.

I leveraged my technical background and skills in this project by testing the Household Metabolism Tracker which collected users' data.
